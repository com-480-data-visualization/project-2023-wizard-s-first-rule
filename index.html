The Dataset

Welcome to our website dedicated to exploring the evolution of video games and its dynamic landscape over time and across different regions. Through a collection of informative graphs, we offer a unique glimpse into the ever-changing world of gaming and the shifting interests within this captivating industry. On this platform, we provide a comprehensive overview of the progression of video games, charting their development from early arcade classics to the intricate and immersive experiences of today. Our graphs highlight the advancements in game mechanics, graphics, and genres, revealing the transformation that has taken place over the years.

Additionally, we delve into the regional variations in video game interests, uncovering intriguing insights into the diverse gaming cultures around the globe. By analyzing popular genres, gaming preferences, and platform choices across different regions, we aim to shed light on how local factors and technological advancements have shaped gaming trends.

Whether you are a gaming enthusiast, a curious observer, or a researcher interested in the evolution of video games, our website provides a data-driven exploration of this ever-evolving industry. Join us as we uncover the stories hidden within the realm of video games and gain a deeper understanding of the fascinating journey it has undertaken.

To have a first glimpse of our data and to get a better understanding of the dataset, we invite you to play with the violin plot below. You can select the category you want to display on the x-axis (platform, genre, publisher) and the displayed value (global sales, NA sales, EU sales, JP sales, other sales) which is shown through the width of the violin plot.

Upon examination, it becomes evident that platform games enjoyed immense popularity during the initial stages of video gaming. However, over time, their prominence has waned. This shift can be attributed to various factors, including the rapid technological advancements within the video game industry. These advancements have introduced new and more immersive gaming experiences, which have influenced changes in player preferences.

Sports:

Sports games simulate various real-life sports, allowing players to engage in virtual versions of popular athletic activities. An example of a popular sports game is "FIFA Soccer" by Electronic Arts, known for its realistic soccer gameplay.

Platform:

Platform games involve navigating through a series of platforms and obstacles to progress through levels. "Super Mario Bros." by Nintendo is an iconic platform game that introduced the world to the adventures of Mario, the famous plumber.

Racing:

Racing games focus on high-speed competition, typically involving vehicles such as cars, motorcycles, or even futuristic spacecraft. "Gran Turismo" by Polyphony Digital is a renowned racing game series known for its realistic driving experience and extensive car selection.

Role-Playing (RPG):

RPGs allow players to assume the roles of fictional characters and embark on immersive quests and adventures in vast, fantasy worlds. "The Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim" by Bethesda Game Studios is an acclaimed RPG known for its expansive open-world exploration and rich storytelling.

Puzzle:

Puzzle games test players' problem-solving skills through challenges that require logic, pattern recognition, or spatial awareness. "Tetris" by Alexey Pajitnov is a classic puzzle game that has remained popular for decades, challenging players to arrange falling blocks into complete lines.

Misc:

Miscellaneous genre represents games that do not fit into a specific category or have unique gameplay elements. These games often offer a blend of different genres or introduce innovative mechanics. "Minecraft" by Mojang Studios is an example of a highly successful game that defies traditional genre classifications, combining elements of sandbox, survival, and creative gameplay.

Shooter:

Shooter games emphasize combat and gunplay, often from a first-person or third-person perspective. The "Call of Duty" series by Activision is a popular example of shooter games known for their intense multiplayer action and realistic military settings.

Simulation:

Simulation games aim to replicate real-life activities or scenarios, allowing players to experience various professions or situations. "The Sims" by Maxis is a widely-known simulation game that lets players create and control virtual people in a simulated world, managing their daily lives and aspirations.

Action:

Action games feature fast-paced gameplay and focus on physical challenges, reflexes, and hand-eye coordination. "Uncharted" by Naughty Dog is a popular action-adventure game that combines exploration, puzzle-solving, and intense action sequences.

Fighting:

Fighting games pit players against each other in one-on-one battles, showcasing martial arts or special abilities. "Street Fighter" by Capcom is a legendary fighting game franchise that has entertained players with its diverse roster of characters and strategic combat.

Adventure:

Adventure games focus on exploration, puzzle-solving, and narrative-driven experiences. "The Legend of Zelda: Breath of the Wild" by Nintendo is an acclaimed adventure game that immerses players in a vast, open-world filled with secrets, quests, and memorable characters.

Strategy:

Strategy games challenge players to plan and execute complex strategies to achieve victory. "Civilization VI" by Firaxis Games is a renowned strategy game where players guide the development of a civilization throughout history, balancing diplomacy, warfare, and cultural growth.


Top selling consoles

Among the top-selling consoles in gaming history, several noteworthy consoles have achieved remarkable success. The PlayStation (PS) series, consisting of the original PlayStation, PlayStation 2 (PS2), and PlayStation 3 (PS3), holds a prominent position in the gaming industry. The PlayStation brand revolutionized gaming experiences, with each iteration pushing boundaries in graphics, gameplay, and immersive storytelling. The PS2, in particular, stands out as the best-selling console of all time, boasting a vast library of games and a loyal fan base.

Another standout console is the Wii, developed by Nintendo. What set the Wii apart was its innovative motion-sensing controller, which introduced a whole new level of interactive gameplay. The Wii's accessibility and family-friendly focus attracted a wide audience, including casual gamers and non-gamers alike. Its success demonstrated the market potential of appealing to a broader demographic beyond traditional gamers.

The Nintendo DS, a handheld console, also made a significant impact. With its dual screens and touch-based interface, the DS offered unique gameplay experiences and a diverse library of titles. It became one of the best-selling handheld consoles, captivating gamers on the go and introducing novel gameplay mechanics.

The Xbox 360 (X360) from Microsoft also played a vital role in shaping the gaming landscape. It gained popularity for its robust online gaming capabilities, a vast array of multimedia features, and a rich library of games. The X360 successfully established the Xbox brand as a major player in the console market and laid the foundation for its subsequent iterations.

These top-selling consoles exemplify the dynamic and competitive nature of the gaming industry. Each console brought its unique features, gaming experiences, and market appeal, leaving a lasting impact on the gaming community. Their success not only speaks to the technological advancements and gaming innovations they introduced but also highlights the diverse interests and preferences of gamers worldwide.

An Ocean of Genres

Video games have become a global phenomenon, captivating audiences around the world. However, when it comes to preferences for video game genres, different countries and cultures often exhibit distinct inclinations. These divergences can be attributed to a variety of factors, including historical context, cultural values, technological advancements, and social dynamics.


The graph you can see below depicts the relative popularity of genres in different regions through time. The height of each section represents the preference for a specific genre, while the countries are arranged along the x-axis. By examining the varying heights of the bars, we can easily discern the dominant genres in each country and identify any significant differences.

It is important to note that these are general trends and preferences can vary greatly within countries and cultures. Moreover, globalization and the accessibility of gaming platforms have led to increased cross-cultural influences, resulting in the blending and adoption of different genres worldwide.


As the gaming industry continues to evolve, it will be fascinating to witness how preferences for video game genres shift and converge across different countries and cultures. This diversity in gaming preferences reflects the beauty of cultural exchange and the globality of the desire for entertainment.
